# 📊 HR Analytics Dashboard | Power BI

## 📌 Project Overview

This HR Analytics Dashboard was developed using Power BI to analyze employee attrition patterns and workforce characteristics. The dashboard transforms raw employee data into meaningful business insights, helping HR teams understand why employees leave and which factors contribute most to attrition.

The dashboard focuses on employee demographics, salary distribution, job satisfaction, overtime impact, department-wise attrition, and education-based attrition analysis.

---

# 🎯 Business Objective

Employee attrition directly impacts organizational productivity, recruitment costs, training expenses, and workforce stability.

This dashboard helps answer key business questions:

* What is the overall attrition rate?
* Which job roles experience the highest attrition?
* Which departments are most affected?
* Does overtime contribute to employee turnover?
* Which salary groups have higher attrition?
* Which age groups are more likely to leave?
* Does educational background influence attrition?

# 📊 Dashboard Visualizations

## 1️⃣ Attrition by Job Role

### Purpose

Analyzes employee turnover across different job roles.

### Business Insight

The highest attrition is observed among:

* Sales Representatives
* Laboratory Technicians
* Human Resources
* Sales Executives

### Why This Visual?

This chart helps identify specific roles where employee retention efforts should be prioritized.

### Business Value

HR teams can create role-specific retention strategies instead of applying generic solutions across the organization.

---

## 2️⃣ Attrition by Department

### Purpose

Shows the proportion of attrition across organizational departments.

### Findings

* Research & Development → 55.88%
* Sales → 39.08%
* Human Resources → 5.04%

### Why This Visual?

The donut chart effectively shows the contribution of each department to total attrition.

### Business Value

Helps management identify departments requiring workforce intervention and retention planning.

---

## 3️⃣ Overtime vs Attrition

### Purpose

Analyzes the relationship between overtime and employee turnover.

### Findings

Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition compared to employees who do not work overtime.

### Why This Visual?

Overtime is a common cause of employee burnout and dissatisfaction.

### Business Value

Supports decisions related to workload balancing and employee well-being initiatives.

---

## 4️⃣ Attrition by Salary Slab

### Purpose

Examines attrition across different salary categories.

### Findings

Highest attrition occurs in:

* Up to 5K salary group
* 10K–15K salary group

Lowest attrition occurs in:

* 15K+ salary group

### Why This Visual?

Compensation is one of the strongest factors affecting employee retention.

### Business Value

Helps management evaluate salary structures and compensation policies.

---

## 5️⃣ Attrition by Age Group

### Purpose

Analyzes employee turnover across age categories.

### Findings

Highest attrition occurs among employees aged:

* 18–25 Years (36%)

Attrition gradually decreases with increasing age.

### Why This Visual?

Different age groups have different career priorities and turnover behavior.

### Business Value

Enables targeted retention strategies for younger employees.

---

## 6️⃣ Attrition by Education Field

### Purpose

Evaluates attrition based on educational background.

### Findings

Higher attrition is observed among employees from:

* Life Sciences
* Medical
* Marketing

Lower attrition is observed among:

* Human Resources
* Other Fields

### Why This Visual?

Educational specialization can influence career opportunities and mobility.

### Business Value

Provides insights into workforce composition and educational trends affecting attrition.

# 🔍 Key Insights

### Employee Retention

* Overall attrition rate is 16.1%.
* Employee turnover remains a significant workforce challenge.

### Job Role Analysis

* Sales Representatives experience the highest attrition.
* Technical and operational roles show moderate turnover.

### Overtime Impact

* Employees working overtime are substantially more likely to leave the organization.

### Compensation Analysis

* Lower salary groups experience higher attrition.
* Competitive compensation improves employee retention.

### Demographic Analysis

* Younger employees (18–25 years) have the highest attrition rate.
* Attrition decreases as employee age increases.

### Department Analysis

* Research & Development contributes the largest share of attrition.
* Sales department also experiences significant employee turnover.

---

# 💡 Recommendations

* Reduce excessive overtime workloads.
* Improve retention programs for younger employees.
* Review compensation structures for lower salary groups.
* Conduct department-specific employee engagement initiatives.
* Implement career growth and development programs.
* Improve job satisfaction through recognition and employee wellness initiatives.
